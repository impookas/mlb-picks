How It Works

Transparent methodology — no black boxes

🎯 The Model

A multi-factor statistical model that analyzes 10 data signals to generate a win probability for every MLB game. We only publish picks where the model finds a 62%+ win probability — about 2 per day. Everything below that threshold is filtered out.

All data comes from the official MLB Stats API. No gut calls, no "expert opinions," no fudging.

📊 Full-Season Backtest

We backtested the model across the entire 2025 MLB season — 2,426 games over 182 days — with zero look-ahead bias:

  • Pitcher stats: Computed from game logs before each game date only. A prediction for June 15 uses only data through June 14.
  • Early-season fallback: For pitchers with fewer than 3 starts in the current season, the model falls back to their prior-year (2024) stats.
  • Team stats: Uses the prior season (2024) as the baseline — no current-year leakage.

Results on 62%+ picks (361 total):

  • Record: 239-122 (66.2% win rate)
  • ROI with realistic moneyline odds: +2.5%
  • Brier Skill: +2.6% (beating coin flip)
  • Calibration at 65%: predicted 64.5%, actual 64.4% — nearly perfect

Every single pick from the backtest is viewable on the Track Record page.

📈 Pitcher Scoring

Starting pitchers are the single biggest factor. Each pitcher gets a composite score (0-100) based on:

  • Season stats: ERA (22%), WHIP (18%), K/BB ratio (15%), K/9 (15%), BB/9 (12%), HR/9 (10%), ground ball rate (8%)
  • Recent form: Last 5 starts, recency-weighted — a pitcher on a hot streak gets more credit than one coasting on April numbers
  • vs. Team history: Career stats against the opposing lineup (15+ IP minimum to filter noise)
  • Regression: Pitchers with fewer than 50 IP are regressed toward league average. Small samples lie.

Season score (60%) and recent form (40%) are blended into one rating. The gap between the two starters is the biggest single driver of the prediction.

💰 ROI Calculation

We don't use flat -110 odds for ROI — that overstates returns on favorites. Our ROI calculation:

  • Converts each pick's probability to an estimated moneyline with standard sportsbook vig (~4.5%)
  • Calculates actual payout based on those odds — a -170 favorite only pays $5.88 on a $10 bet
  • Tracks cumulative profit/loss across all picks

This is more conservative than most handicapping sites report, but it's honest.

📊 How We Measure Accuracy

Win-loss record alone is misleading. We track:

  • Brier Score: How close predicted probabilities are to outcomes. 0.25 = coin flip. Lower = better.
  • Brier Skill: Percentage improvement over always guessing 50/50. Positive = the model adds value.
  • Calibration: When we say 65%, does it actually win ~65% of the time? The Track Record page shows this directly.
  • ROI (Real Odds): Simulated profit with realistic moneyline odds and vig.

🕒 Daily Process

  • 10:00 AM ET — Model pulls all available data, scores every game, publishes 62%+ picks
  • 1:00 AM ET — Results scored automatically, Track Record updated
  • All picks are timestamped and locked before first pitch — no retroactive edits, ever
  • Some days there are 0 picks. If the model doesn't see an edge, it doesn't force one.

💡 What This Is (And Isn't)

This is a data-driven edge-finding tool. It identifies games where the model sees a higher probability than the market implies, and publishes them transparently.

It is not a guaranteed money printer. Over 2,426 backtested games, the model's 62%+ picks hit at 66.2% and produced a modest +2.5% ROI against realistic odds. That's real signal, but it's not going to make you rich overnight.

The Track Record page shows every pick — wins and losses — with no editing or cherry-picking. If the model stops working, you'll see it there first.
